act 01. Protector's death

scroll to read more

Once upon a time, in a faraway place that no ordinary man could reach, an old village existed under rules of Werewolf Board-game. Each person there, other than being their-original-selves, was destined to play a role in the game.

Harry, the beautiful, jobless protagonist, woke up from a coma, losing half of his memories. Walking out the square after one month, hearing the villagers fussing about “moon” “month” “wolf” “death”, Harry didn’t get a thing.

On his way back home, a strange door showed up with the sign WONDERLAND. A hunch urged Harry to walk through that door.

WONDERLAND was a bar, and its bartender was Alice – Harry’s best friend.

Alice helped Harry recall his memories of the Werewolf rules:

01. It happens once a month, when the moon is full. The next noon is the execution hours.

02. The wolves kill one person each full-moon night, two at blood-moon night.

03. There are eight characters left who can decide the outcome, and two wolves hiding in between:

Gotham – the mayor
May – the healer
Richman – the landlord
Roxie – the candy dealer
Layla – the weaver
Bonnie – the shepherdess
Alice – the bartender
Harry – the unemployed

04. No one can escape. The only way out is to kill all the wolves before they eat us all.

05. Harry’s role, in case he forgot it as well, was the Normal Villager – totally useless!

Leaving Wonderland, Harry encountered Roxie, who was insisting on a compensation from Bonnie, who just bumped into Roxie’s candy basket. He defended Bonnie, and accordingly, created a grudge with Roxie.

They met auntie May on a rush to pick the wormwood – the leaves believed to heal wolf-bites. Harry, regarding May as his savior, volunteered to help.

Meanwhile, the villagers and Layla were gossiping about Richman, who the whole village conceded as one of the two wolves, though no one was brave enough to denounce him.

And they were exposed – by Richman.

Richman became frightened after mishearing that conversation.

He had to come up with a plan – an early move to help him win the game sooner.
“The Witch – poison – liquid – wine – Alice in Wonderland!”

With that outstanding deduction, Richman rushed back to the bar and compelled Alice to use up her killing and healing potions on Roxie and Harry with his tempting reasons.

The deal settled, in seconds. The moon was full that night.

The next day, surprisingly, both Harry and Roxie were still alive. But the strange thing is that they bumped into each other so many times at the square.
And everyone else around them talked about love.

Alice was the Cupid.

And the linked Couple cannot be more than 10 feet apart if they haven’t revealed their roles for each other.

Roxie blew up and ran away. Harry ran, of course, after her.

The reluctant “lovebirds” continued their argument on the boat that took Roxie to her home in the middle of a river.

Harry even tried to threaten Roxie by throwing her oar away, but the boat weirdly moved itself to a cavern, where the magic happened.

Roxie was the real Witch, and she despised the villagers because of their selfishness.

Harry was too stunned to utter a word until the boat landed.

But he managed to persuade Roxie to side up with him in the end.

One month after, in the full-moon night, Richman was murdered by Roxie’s toxic candy.

At least, it was not an awful death.

“One wolf left to win!”

The next morning, Layla was found dead at the square.

The execution hours reached nowhere, because no one has clear proof.

Another month passed, in the full-moon night, the Couple tried to connect the evidence they gathered to find out the second wolf – one out of three suspects: Bonnie, Gotham, May.

After a while, Harry admitted that he witnessed something the night Richman died in Wonderland: Layla and Bonnie were there, and Bonnie was trying to find a dubious thing in Richman’s body.

What a proof!

The Couple jumped to a conclusion: the shepherdess is a wolf.

Suddenly, Alice arrived: “Bonnie was attacked by the wolf... But auntie May helped heal and brought her home.”

All the traces messed up. Harry decided to go to Bonnie’s place for more explicit clues from her.

3 A.M. Bonnie noticed Harry coming from distance, her face blushed then brightened.
She confessed to him, about her memorabilia locked at Richman’s when he was living, about how she was healed, and about her special admiration for Harry.
In the story, an unprecedented name popped up: ‘Forest Wolf leaf’ – clearly written on a potion in May’s house.

Out of nowhere, there was Gotham sitting on the veranda, smoking a pipe, and watching the young.
Then, the old man handed Harry a book named “1001 exclusive herbs for Wolves.” Afterwards, Harry might have realized the answer he was chasing.

When Harry was gone, Bonnie’s smile dimmed. She went back to her dream, her unrequited love for him.

“There should be a little sacrifice for a greater great.”

Roxie raged at the news. The Trio decided to sneak into May’s house and find the ‘Forest Wolf leaf’ potion.
It was there – everything was clear.
The execution was coming, they had to go.

Harry didn’t readily accept the truth, for May was his savior.
Nonetheless, he was a justice advocate, so he had to make up his mind and follow his forever righteousness.

Execution hours got tense.

Roxie, Harry, and Alice took their turns to denounce May’s guilt.

“Good job, Harry. Remember: I never wanted to save you in the first place. I have nothing more to say, it should be the endgame now.”

After 90 seconds for self-defense, May received a black sea of thumbs-down from the villagers.

May died on the hanging truss.

“The Villagers won! LET’S CELEBRATE!”

Through the ups and downs, it seemed Alice’s love potions finally came into effect and made the Couple truly fall in love.

Roxie accepted Harry’s invite to come back to the village and live with him.

“Our story has come to an end now. Wish you all a happy full-moon season!”

That night, the blood moon rose.

Bonnie ran to Harry’s house in tears and informed the Couple: Alice was attacked by a wolf.

THERE. WAS. A. WOLF. LEFT.

Roxie immediately took out the healing candy and Harry brought it to save Alice.

“It is not Gotham...” Alice cried out her breathy words.

It was Bonnie.
When Harry ran back home, Bonnie welcomed him. A knife in her right hand, a rope tying Roxie in her left hand.
Bonnie was the Cursed Wolf – May put the curse on her.

At that moment, Bonnie exposed every secret that all the villagers – including Roxie and Alice – had been hiding from Harry.
That Harry was the Prince, who had double lives, who was destined to bring peace to the Village, and who was hung on the noose once by the villagers he always trusted. Therefore, May was never his savior: his role saved him.
That Harry and Bonnie were lovers, which he completely forgot after the coma. And Bonnie’s half-moon necklace was Harry’s gift.
That Bonnie never bore a thought about the wolves’ plan, for all she wanted was Harry’s love.

But Harry didn’t relent, though his heart was heavily wounded by his most sacred ideal.

“Then let me make you feel how I felt.”

Bonnie was about to kill Roxie when Alice arrived: “They are linked by the Cupid. If Roxie dies, so does Harry.”

Bonnie stopped, bursting out her most painful laugh: “Turns out it was all a play you damned people created. And I am the only one who was betrayed.”
“All too well! Please bask in your triumph, your despicable triumph.”
Bonnie turned around, and killed Alice, a second time.

Execution hours. Harry, Roxie, Gotham, and Bonnie on the hanging truss.

Two thumb-downs from the Couple.
One thumb-up from Bonnie.
One thumb-up from Gotham.

Richman was the Betrayer, and Gotham was the last last Wolf.
The Couple fell down. The Wolves won.

End.
